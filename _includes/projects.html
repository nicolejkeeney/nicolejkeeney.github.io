Understanding Changes in Global Extreme Precipitation Using Machine Learning - Climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of hydroclimatic extremes--such as drought, flooding, and extreme precipitation-- around the globe. As part of my graduate research at Colorado State University, I am studying the underlying physical mechanisms that contribute to extreme precipitation in different regions around the globe. This work involves training a series of machine learning models to learn the atmospheric circulation patterns associated with extreme precipitation events. I'll eventually use these trained models to study extreme precipitation in the future using climate model projections. Stay tuned-- I hope to present my preliminary results at the American Geophysical Union's 2024 Fall Meeting!
